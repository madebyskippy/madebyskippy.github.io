City Explorer - 2018



I was an Associate Developer at Potion Design, and covered a range of responsibilities, from concept to development to deployment. I worked closely with designers to build tools and implement different assets and animations, combining them with procedural movement to be responsive to user input. I built systems for interactive projection mapping and experimented with different physical controls.

Some of the projects I worked on are yet unreleased. Please contact me for more details.


One of the projects I worked on was the City Explorer, for the NYU Langone Hassenfeld Children's Hospital. It won the SEGD Honor Award and the Core77 Notable Interaction Award.

City Explorer is a collection of NYC-themed gestural games for up to six people at a time. Each game is designed to last a short while and be easy to come in and out of, to accomodate different people's interests.

I developed several of the games, including a crowd favorite dancing costume game, a free flying game where you can soar around Manhattan with giant landmarks, and a set of skyline games where you can paint, knock around cloud animals, and set off fireworks.
